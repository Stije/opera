Open Platform for Evaluation of Repurposed Anti-infectives

The Challenge

When infection occurs, pathogen load increases rapidly in the body, reaching peak levels within days of symptom onset.

Untreated Population

Without effective treatment, some acute infections clear slowly through natural immune responses. However, in severe infections like malaria, pathogen loads can continue to increase when left untreated, leading to life-threatening outcomes.

Treated Population

At the critical moment, treatment is administered. With treatment, pathogen clearance is rapid. The steep initial decline in the log curve reflects the direct drug effect on pathogen replication.

The Drug Effect

The relative difference in the log-linear slope of pathogen clearance rate between the treated (green) and untreated (red) populations represents the drug effect. The steeper decline in the treated curve directly measures the therapeutic impact on pathogen dynamics.

The OPERA Initiative

Our research focuses on optimizing treatment strategies by understanding pathogen clearance dynamics. A key requirement for advancing anti-infective drugs through clinical development is demonstrating their ability to accelerate pathogen clearance in humans.
